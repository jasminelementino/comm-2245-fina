Education

When I think of my education I think of my most recent years, i.e., high school and early years of college. I never think of the earlier years that contributed to my progress than I was able to achieve later on.

In elementary school I didn’t care much about school. Although the memories are fond, I remember that I would never be as excited about the lessons as I was about the activities that the lessons entailed or activities we were rewarded with in between lessons. I didn’t pay much attention to tests and filled in the answer bubbles as if it were a game. However, I made friends during these crucial years of life that would be a great influence on me for the rest of my life regarding my education.

Middle school is the least rememberable part of my life. But, I will always have gratitude towards my eighth-grade self for deciding to enroll in a dual credit program for my high school years. If it weren’t for her (she didn’t think that she could do it in the first place and was very hesitant) I would not have accomplished what I have.

In high school, after much crucial development, I finally began to take school seriously. Because I took dual enrollment classes throughout the entirety of high school I was able to graduate with two associate degrees which gave me a head start in my career and in college.

To put it shortly, my most notable educational accomplishment thus far is graduating with an associate degree in general studies and liberal arts. My goal is to graduate with a BA in political science and communication with a minor in psychology.
